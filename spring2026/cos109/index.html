University Catalogue Description

A continuation of COS 108 with an extensive study of the manipulation of arrays, strings, record structure, and files. A brief introduction to Object Oriented Programming is provided.

Course Objectives

This course serves as the introduction to the Object-Oriented Programming model. We will cover programming techniques to manipulate members of C++'s string class, arrays and pointers. Additionally, object-oriented programming techniques will be introduced. Students will understand the goals and concepts of object-oriented design such as abstraction, encapsulation, code re-use, inheritance, structure hiding, and polymorphism.

Course Specific Requirements, Expectations, Policies

Class Attendance

Students are expected to attend the entire period of each class throughout the semester. The university attendance policy is described in the 2022-2023 Kentucky State University Handbook (https://tinyurl.com/2022-2023student). Students with service-related absences (military, on or off campus representation of the University, jury-duty) and excused absences (documented illness, documented illness or death of immediate family, court appearances, or university sponsored events/trips) should file documentation with the Dean of Student Leadership, Conduct, and Health in the Office of Student Engagement and Campus Life, housed in the Hill Student Center, Office 334.

Unexcused absences: To help maintain a smooth learning environment, students who arrive more than five (5) minutes late to start of class AND OR leaving class early will be marked as ‘Absent’. Each three (3) unexcused absences will be a drop in a letter grade for the course. If you have any form of note or excuse IT must be submitted to Student Services for review and dispersion! No exceptions!

Cell Phone Policy

To create a focused and respectful learning environment, all cell phones, PDAs, beepers, and similar electronic devices must be turned off and put away during class. Texting is not permitted at any time during class. Failure to follow this policy will result in an unexcused absence for that day.

Headphones, earbuds, and music-playing devices may not be worn or used during class. During exams, no cell phones may be on desks or visible at any time; the presence of a cell phone during a test will result in a zero for that exam.

Assignments

Classwork and homework may be assigned and may serve as the basis for in-class discussions. To support meaningful learning, students are expected to come to class prepared and engaged, with the goal of developing a thorough understanding of the course material. A due date will be provided on Canvas or announced in class for each weekly homework assignment. All assignments are due by the end of the day on the posted due date. Please pay close attention to assignment and exam deadlines. In general, deadlines will not be extended, and late submissions are not accepted.

Generally, assignments are required to be submitted to Canvas. Do not use KSU email to email/submit your work.

Excused Absences

For any excused absence, all make-up assignments must be completed within one week.

Student Responsibilities

If you need to miss a class, please remember that you are responsible for keeping up with course expectations. This includes finding out what assignments were given, noting any announced due dates, and reviewing the material covered during your absence.

Academic Honesty

All students must conduct their studies at KSU honestly, ethically and in accordance with accepted standards of academic conduct. See the Academic Conduct section of the 2022-2023 KSU Student Handbook (tinyurl.com/2022-2023student) (pp. 19-21). Cheating and plagiarism will result in a score of zero on that particular test or assignment and thus lowering of grade in the course.

  • Cheating: Cheating is defined as the fraudulent or deceptive taking, giving, or presenting of any information or material with the intent of aiding oneself or another on any academic work which is considered in any way in the determination of a course grade. Cheating includes but is not limited to copying a hand-in assignment, looking on another person’s exam during a test, signing in for someone else, inappropriate use of technology during an exam (like having a cell phone on during a test), and the un-permitted use of “cheat sheets” or using internet sites.

    Copying and pasting from the internet is considered cheating as you are using the intellectual property of someone else and turning it in as you own work. In addition, the use of AI to complete assignments and or submissions will not be tolerated, except for special circumstances explicitly stated earlier, any use of AI tools must be fully disclosed.

    Cheating may result in an F in the class. It may also result in expulsion from the university.

    In addition,

    • Documentation of cheating will be provided by the instructor to the school chair, college dean, and registrar for implementing into student record. It will be up to the instructor if a second attempt is made possible for completing that exam, assignment or activity.
    • Bathroom breaks will not be allowed during exams/tests. Any student leaving the classroom or lab during an exam/test. The exam/test will be turned in with no additional time or retake.
    • No cell phones, ear buds, earmuffs, large coats, head coverings, caps or hats will be allowed during exams/tests.
    • Not following these stipulations, you may be asked to leave the classroom or lab.
  • Plagiarism: Plagiarism is the intentional or unintentional act of submitting the work of another as one’s own. It includes the submission of the complete or partial work, or of the words, ideas, or format of another, published or unpublished, without appropriate reference and source credit. It includes employing or permitting another person to produce, alter, or revise material which the student submits as his or her own. If the student has any uncertainty regarding plagiarism in the submission of any material to his or her instructor, he or she should discuss the matter with the instructor prior to submission of the material.
  • Forgery: Forgery is falsely recording the signature of an advisor, instructor, or any university official on any official University academic document.
